    Relation, preorder #

    Relation #

    Definition. A relation over a set \(S\) is a set of [tuples] over \(S\) with the same cardinality:

    • a binary relation is a set of [pairs] of elements of \(S\) or equivalently a subset of \(S \times S\)
    • a ternary relation is a set of [triples] of elements of \(S\) or equivalently a subset of \(S \times S \times S\)
    • etc.

    For instance, if \(S = \{a,b,c\}\) , then

    • \(\{ (a,b), (b,a), (a,a) \}\) is a binary relation over \(S\)

    • \(\{ (a,b,a), (c,c,c) \}\) is a ternary relation over \(S\)

    Binary relation #

    A binary relation can be represented in multiple ways.

    In particular, it can be represented as a (directed) graph (and conversely).

    For instance

    A binary relation can also be represented with an infix symbol.

    For instance the relation

    \(\{ (a,a), (a,b), (b,a) \}\)

    can be represented as

    \(a \preceq a\) ,

    \(a \preceq b\) and

    \(b \preceq a\)

    Reflexive, transitive, antisymmetric #

    Definition. A binary relation \(\preceq\) over \(S\) is

    • reflexive if \(a \preceq a\) for all \(a \in S\)
    • transitive if for all \(a, b, c \in S\)

    \(\qquad \qquad \qquad a \preceq b\) and \(b \preceq c\) imply \(a \preceq c\)

    • antisymmetric if no two elements are equal w.r.t. \(\preceq\) i.e. if for all \(a, b \in S\)

    \( \qquad \qquad \qquad a \preceq b\) and \(b \preceq a\) imply \(a = b\)

    Preorder, order #

    Definition. A binary relation is a preorder if it is reflexive and transitive.

    It is an order if it is also antisymmetric.

    Total and partial #

    A preorder over \(S\) is total if every two elements of \(S\) are comparable, i.e. if

    \(a \preceq b\) or \(b \preceq a\) (or both) for all \(a, b \in S\) .

    A preoder that is not total is called partial. \({(a,a), (a,b), (b,a)}\)

    Example. The natural order \(\le\) over real numbers is a total order (i.e. total, reflexive, transitive and antisymmetric).

    Example. If \(S\) is a set, then the set inclusion relation \(\subseteq\) over the power set of \(S\) i>s a partial order (i.e. reflexive, transitive and antisymmetric).

    If \(S\) is a set, then a total preorder is intuitively any relation that allows sorting \(S\) .

    Example. Let \(S\) be a set of people, and let \(\preceq\) be the relation defined over \(S\) by

    \(p_1 \preceq p_2\) iff
    \(p_1\) is younger than (or as old as) \(p_2\) .

    Then \(\preceq\) is a total preorder (i.e. total reflexive and transitive) but it is not an order (i.e. not antisymmetric, because two people can have the same age).

    Warning. The term “order” is often used to refer to a total order.

    Lexicographic product #

    If \(\preceq_o\) is a preorder, let us use:

    • \(a \prec_o b\) as a shortcut for \(a \preceq_o b\) and \(b \not\preceq_o a\)
    • \(a =\text{o} b\) as a shortcut for \(a \preceq_o b\) and \(b \preceq_o a\) .

    The lexicographic product \(\preceq\) of a peorders \(\preceq_1\) by a preprder \(\preceq_2\) is defined by

    \(a \preceq b\) iff \(a \prec_1 b\) or ( \(a =\_o b\) and \(a \preceq_2 b\) )

    Example. Let \(S\) be a set of people, and let \(\preceq*{\text{age}}\) and \(\preceq*{\text{size}}\) be the total preorders defined over \(S\) by

    \(p*1 \preceq*{age} p_2\) iff
    \(p_1\) is younger than (or as old as) \(p_2\) .

    \(p*1 \preceq*{size} p_2\) iff
    \(p_1\) is smaller than (or as tall as) \(p_2\) .

    Then the product \preceq of \preceq*{age} by \preceq*{size} is defined by

    \(p_1 \preceq p_2\) iff
    \(p_1\) is younger than \(p_2\) or

    Warning The lexicographic product of \(\preceq_1\) by \(\preceq_2\) may differ from the lexicographic product of \(\preceq_2\) by \(\preceq_1\) .

    Hint. The lexicographic product of two total preorders (resp. total order) is itself a total preorder (resp. total order).
